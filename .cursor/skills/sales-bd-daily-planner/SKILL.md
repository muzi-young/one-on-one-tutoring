# Sales BD Daily Planner

## Purpose

Create a practical daily operating system for software business managers: clear priorities, faster sales execution, and stronger process control.

## Daily Cadence

1. Start of day (15 min): define 1-3 revenue outcomes, collect all tasks, assign each task to a quadrant.
2. Morning focus block (60-90 min): complete Q2 high-impact work before meetings.
3. Midday checkpoint (10 min): re-rank priorities based on client updates and internal dependencies.
4. Afternoon execution blocks: close follow-ups, proposal progress, and cross-team alignment.
5. End-of-day review (15 min): update pipeline, confirm next actions, and schedule tomorrow.

## Eisenhower Quadrant Rules

- Q1 (Urgent + Important): client risk, contract deadlines, critical blockers. Do now.
- Q2 (Not Urgent + Important): prospecting, proposal quality, strategic account planning. Schedule deep-work blocks.
- Q3 (Urgent + Not Important): interrupt-driven requests, low-value admin tasks. Delegate, batch, or time-box.
- Q4 (Not Urgent + Not Important): low-impact activities. Remove or defer.

Guardrails:
- Keep at least 40 percent of focused time for Q2 work.
- If Q1 keeps growing, perform root-cause analysis and add prevention actions.

## Sales Process Management

Track every active opportunity with stage, exit criteria, and next action:

1. Lead: ideal customer profile fit and contact validity confirmed.
2. Qualified: pain, budget, authority, and timeline documented.
3. Solution Fit: demo/discovery completed and decision criteria captured.
4. Proposal: commercial proposal sent with a scheduled follow-up.
5. Negotiation: objections and legal/commercial items tracked to closure.
6. Closed Won/Lost: outcome reason logged and post-action plan recorded.

Mandatory fields per opportunity:
- owner
- current stage
- next action
- due date
- risk level
- confidence level

## Escalation Rules

- No progress for more than 7 days: escalate and define a recovery action.
- Opportunity stuck in one stage beyond threshold: trigger manager review.
- High-value deal with unclear decision process: create an executive alignment plan.
